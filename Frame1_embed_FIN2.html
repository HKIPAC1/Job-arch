Job Architecture Framework

Helen Keller Intl uses a unified 15-grade structure designed to provide clarity of progression, enable mobility across functions and geographies, differentiate scope, impact, and accountability, and ensure consistent expectations across country and global offices.

Role clusters — select to explore
Two layers of expectations
Layer 1 — Core values
Our four values — Rigor, Integrity, Courage, and Compassion — are the threshold of belonging at Helen Keller Intl. They apply at every grade and in every role, assessed as demonstrated consistently or not demonstrated consistently.
Layer 2 — Behavioural competencies
Four skill-based competencies that grow with scope, practice, training, and experience. Assessed as Developing, Proficient, or Outstanding. Competencies are grouped by role cluster and evolve across grades.
Our four core values
Rigor
Delivering thoughtful, accurate work grounded in evidence. Looking for ways to learn and improve, not just complete tasks.
Integrity
Acting honestly even when it is difficult. Keeping commitments and speaking truthfully about outcomes.
Courage
Naming problems directly. Offering solutions and standing by principled decisions, even under pressure.
Compassion
Actively listening and treating colleagues, partners, and communities we serve with genuine respect, dignity, and care.
Grade progression
Each grade has three sub-levels reflecting depth, consistency, and autonomy within the same scope — Associate (developing within grade scope), Core (fully operating at grade expectations), and Senior (sustained mastery at grade level). Supervisory responsibilities begin at Grade 7. Executive leadership begins at Grade 13.

Competencies are grouped by role cluster. Select a cluster to review the full expectations for each proficiency level.
